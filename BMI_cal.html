Understanding Body Mass Index (BMI)

BMI is a simple screening tool that uses your height and weight to estimate body fat. While it's a quick starting point, it has significant limitations for active individuals.

📐
What Is BMI?
BMI calculates your weight relative to your height. It's used worldwide as a quick screening method for weight categories.
🎯
Why It Matters
BMI correlates with health risks. Higher BMI is linked to increased risk of heart disease and diabetes.
⚠️
Limitations
BMI doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat. Athletes often appear "overweight" due to dense muscle mass.
💡
Best Used With
Combine BMI with body fat percentage and performance metrics for a complete health picture.

🛑 Important Disclaimer: BMI Accuracy

BMI is a mathematical estimate and **not** a direct measurement of body fat. It cannot differentiate between lean muscle mass and adipose tissue (fat). For athletes, weightlifters, or those with high bone density, a "high" BMI may be a sign of peak physical condition rather than a health risk. Conversely, those with very low muscle mass and higher fat percentages may have a "normal" BMI but high health risks.
