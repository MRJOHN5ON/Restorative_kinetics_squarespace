Where this fits in

Integration, not isolation

Hands-on work is most useful when it supports your training—not when it stands alone. I use it to address limits that get in the way of how you move, so you can train more effectively and feel better doing it. The goal is always: move better, build resilience.

How massage fits into your training

Manual work can support you in three clear ways—assessment, mobility, and recovery. Pick what matters most for where you are right now.

Assessment

Hands-on evaluation helps find tissue restrictions, asymmetries, and compensation patterns—so we can make better decisions about what to train and how.

Mobility support

Targeted soft tissue work can improve range of motion and prep your body so your movement training is more effective.

Recovery

Strategic manual work between sessions can support recovery and keep tissue quality up when training is intense.
